# Property Management Suite (PMS) Brand Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-12  
**Scope:** Marketing materials, runbooks, and documentation in `pms-master`

## 1) Canonical Brand Identity

- **Primary product name:** **Property Management Suite**
- **Short form:** **PMS** (introduce once as `Property Management Suite (PMS)`, then use `PMS`)
- **Audience framing:** Property managers, owners, operations/security teams, and tenants
- **Tone:** Professional, direct, operationally clear, outcome-focused

## 2) Voice & Tone Rules

- Prefer plain, specific language over hype.
- Lead with outcomes and operational impact.
- Keep runbook language imperative and action-oriented.
- Avoid slang, sarcasm, or ambiguous phrasing in procedures.

## 3) Naming & Terminology Standards

- Use **Property Management Suite (PMS)** consistently.
- Keep role names consistent:
  - `Property Manager`
  - `Owner`
  - `Tenant`
  - `Security Team` / `Operations Team`
- Use `rekey` and `key rotation` consistently in security docs.
- Use `Proof Pack` as a branded closeout artifact term where applicable.

## 4) Heading & Formatting Standards

- Use Title Case for section headings.
- Use short purpose sections up front (`Purpose`, `Objective`, `Trigger`, `Expected Outcome`).
- Use numbered procedures for operator actions.
- Use checklists for pre-flight and post-flight controls.

## 5) CTA Language (Marketing Docs)

- Use direct action CTAs:
  - `Start Your Free Trial`
  - `Watch the 2-Minute AI Demo`
  - `Approve / Deny`
- Prefer one primary CTA and one secondary CTA where practical.

## 6) Consistency Guardrails

- Do not alter technical/factual meaning during branding edits.
- Do not edit binary deliverables (PDFs); edit source markdown/docs instead.
- If a brand rule is uncertain, follow dominant repository usage and document the assumption in `BRANDING_UPDATE_SUMMARY.md`.
